踏聲樂章｜EchoCycle

公共藝術聲音互動裝置 · 國立成功大學科技藝術碩士學位學程

作品簡介 · Introduction

《踏聲樂章》以「低碳行動」為核心隱喻：觀眾的踩踏被轉譯為能量的聲光回饋，每一台腳踏車如同一件樂器，既可獨奏亦可合奏；中央「概念樹」以燈光匯聚眾人能量，象徵人與環境的共振循環。作品結合 TouchDesigner 聲光資料流、Phidgets 感測、DMX 燈控與多聲道音訊，回應「在公共空間如何以藝術化方式理解永續」。

EchoCycle transforms human motion into a symphony of light and sound. Each bicycle acts as an individual instrument, resonating with the central "conceptual tree," symbolizing the cycle of human and environmental harmony. Integrating TouchDesigner, Phidgets, DMX, and multi-channel sound design, the work explores how sustainability can be perceived through interactive art.

音樂設計 · Music Design

音樂概念：在待機模式中，音樂與概念樹的「呼吸燈」對應，營造出大自然寧靜卻充滿生命力的氛圍。音樂中融入了台灣特有鳥類的鳴聲，使聆聽體驗更具在地特色與自然感。此外，音樂中時而響起「學校鐘聲」的弦律，為環境增添一種熟悉而溫暖的記憶感。整體設計以 10 分鐘為一循環單位，不斷重複播放，帶來持續、柔和的氛圍。騎乘模式時，展覽共設置 7 台腳踏車，分別對應七種不同樂器。隨機一台腳踏車有民眾使用時，會立即與待機音樂做切換。每一台腳踏車可觸發樂器的三種速度（節奏）變化。隨著後輪轉速不同，概念樹與腳踏車底座的閃燈會與音樂同步加快或放慢。

Music Concept: In standby mode, the music synchronizes with the Concept Tree’s “breathing lights,” creating an atmosphere of natural serenity and vitality. The soundscape incorporates calls of Taiwan’s native birds, enhancing the local and organic texture. Intermittently, the melody of a familiar school bell emerges, evoking warmth and nostalgia. The entire composition loops every 10 minutes, providing a continuous, gentle soundscape. During riding mode, seven bicycles correspond to seven different instruments. Once a bicycle is activated, it switches seamlessly from the ambient track to interactive sound. Each bike triggers three rhythm variations, and as the wheel speed changes, both the Concept Tree and the LED bases flash in sync with the music tempo.

永續與再利用 · Sustainability & Reuse

以工業規格的模矩化設計尺寸設計，低度改裝腳踏車。在展覽後，腳踏車可恢復正常使用，感測器與線材可重複運用於後續專案，鋁擠型結構可再製為書櫃或置物架，玻璃搭配 IKEA 桌腳即可成為書桌。此結構設計不僅呼應「低碳與再利用」的永續展覽宣言，也實踐了藝術裝置的循環再生理念。

Built with modular industrial design standards, the installation allows all components to be reused: bicycles can return to daily use, sensors and wiring can support future projects, aluminum framing can be repurposed into furniture, and glass panels can transform into desks. This design embodies circular sustainability both conceptually and physically.
